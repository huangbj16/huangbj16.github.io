About me

Hi there, I'm Bingjian (Alex) Huang

Currently I'm a senior student studying Computer Science and Technology at Tsinghua University, Beijing, China.

I have great interest in understanding human beings, especially human's interactions with "things". I would like to know how people interact with their daily-life devices, what are the existing limits, and what we can do improve them. That's why I step into the field of Human-Computer Interaction(HCI), which is also called Human-Centered Design(HCD).

I joined the Key Laboratary of Pervasive Computing at Tsinghua University in April, 2018. My advisors are Prof. Yuanchun Shi and Prof. Chun Yu. Since then, I have participated in two research projects related to Virtual Reality(VR), Telepresence, Wearable Devices, and Gesture Design.

Last Summer (July--Septempber, 2019), I worked as a summer intern in Inteco Lab (Advisor: Prof. Mark W. Newman) at School of Information, University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. I collaborated with PhD student Xinghui Yan on a research project about low-burden in-situ self-reporting tools. Our paper was submitted to CHI' 2020.
